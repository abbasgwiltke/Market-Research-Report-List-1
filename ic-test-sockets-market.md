<p><p>The IC test sockets market comprises various types, including Ball Grid Array (BGA), Land Grid Array (LGA), and Quad Flat No-lead (QFN) sockets, each designed for specific packaging technologies. BGA sockets accommodate chips with solder balls, ensuring reliable connections for high-density applications. LGA sockets feature flat pads for testing, suitable for larger ICs. QFN sockets support chips with reduced footprint and no leads, emphasizing efficiency in compact designs. Other sockets cater to diverse configurations, enhancing flexibility in semiconductor testing.</p></p>
