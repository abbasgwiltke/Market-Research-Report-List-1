<p><ul><li>Sea Salt</li><li>Lake Salt</li><li>Well and Rock Salt</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The raw salt market comprises various types, each sourced differently. Sea salt is obtained through evaporation of seawater, known for its minerals and flavor. Lake salt arises from saline lakes and has similar characteristics. Well salt is extracted from underground brine via drilling, often used in industrial applications. Rock salt, mined from geological deposits, is a pure form of sodium chloride, primarily for de-icing and food preservation. Each type serves distinct purposes across culinary, industrial, and health sectors.</p></p>
