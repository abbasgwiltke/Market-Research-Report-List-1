<p><p>The nickel market encompasses various types of ore processing, with nickeliferous limonite and garnierite being two key classifications. Nickeliferous limonite smelting typically involves extracting nickel from low-grade ores, commonly sourced from lateritic deposits, utilizing pyrometallurgical methods. In contrast, garnierite smelting focuses on higher-grade ores rich in magnesium silicate, often favored for its higher nickel content. Both processes cater to the growing demand for nickel, driven by industries such as electric vehicles and stainless steel production.</p></p>
