<p><p>The edible packaging film market is categorized into four main types: proteins, polysaccharides, lipids, and others. Protein-based films, derived from sources like gelatin or soy, offer excellent barrier properties. Polysaccharide films, made from materials like starch or alginate, are biodegradable and provide moisture resistance. Lipid films, often extracted from fats and oils, enhance the shelf-life of products by reducing oxygen permeability. The "others" category includes blends or innovative materials that combine different biopolymers for enhanced functionality and sustainability.</p></p>
